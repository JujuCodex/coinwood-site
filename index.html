How it works

The eco-fee is enforced by Solana's official Token-2022 standard — by the blockchain itself, not by custom code. Every transfer of $CWD automatically sets aside 4%, redistributed every week from a public multisig treasury.

1

You trade $CWD

Buy, sell or transfer $CWD on Solana. A 4% fee is withheld automatically by the token program on each transfer.

2

The fee is split

1.5% reforestation fund, 1% liquidity pool, 1% marketing, 0.5% buyback & burn.

3

Weekly distribution

Withheld fees are harvested every week to a transparent multisig treasury. Every movement is public and verifiable on-chain.

4

Trees get planted

The reforestation fund buys real trees through Tree-Nation. Each planting is certified and visible in the public CoinWood forest.

    Transparency, not promises

    🔒 Standard code only

    $CWD uses Solana's official Token-2022 program — no custom smart contract, nothing exotic to audit. The eco-fee, the fixed supply and the fee cap are enforced by the same code that secures the whole Solana ecosystem.

    🏛️ Public treasury

    Collected fees land in a multisig treasury. Weekly distributions (trees, liquidity, burn) are published and verifiable by anyone, on-chain.

    🌳 Certified planting

    Trees are planted through Tree-Nation, with public certificates in the CoinWood forest. Impact you can click, not a slide in a deck.

    FAQ

    What is CoinWood?

    CoinWood ($CWD) is a token on the Solana blockchain whose core mechanism funds reforestation: 4% of every transaction is automatically set aside, and the largest share buys real, certified trees through Tree-Nation.

    How can I trust the eco-fee?

    The fee is a native feature of Solana's Token-2022 standard, enforced by the blockchain itself. Collected fees go to a public multisig treasury, and every distribution is visible on-chain. Tree plantings are certified by Tree-Nation on a public page.

    When is the presale?

    The public presale opens after the relaunch, on an established launchpad with buyer vesting and automatic liquidity locking. Dates are announced on Telegram and X first — from these official accounts only.

    Is the contract audited?

    There is nothing custom to audit: $CWD deliberately uses only standard, battle-tested Solana programs (Token-2022, established vesting and locking services). We prefer verifiable facts to audit badges.

    What happened to the 2025 token?

    A first version of $CWD was deployed in March 2025 on the classic SPL standard, which cannot carry an on-chain eco-fee. It was never sold or listed. The relaunch uses a new Token-2022 contract so the eco-fee is enforced by the blockchain — the old address is deprecated.

    Is $CWD an investment?

    $CWD is a utility token supporting a reforestation ecosystem. Cryptocurrencies are volatile and you can lose what you put in. Nothing on this site is financial advice — never spend money you cannot afford to lose.
